Research experience can take many forms, from course-based research experience, to campus programs, to intensive summer internships at national laboratories. **Research-focused programs often provide hands-on training, professional development, mentorship, and exposure to potential career paths in research**. This page lists some research-focused programs at UCSB and elsewhere (please note, however, _**this is by no means an exhaustive list**_).

Many of these programs require an application and have specific eligibility requirements based on factors such as academic level, field of study, career interests, and/or research goals. Application timelines also vary, with some opportunities accepting applications nearly a year in advance. We encourage students to explore programs early and plan accordingly

_**Disclaimer:** Program information on this page is current as of Sep 2026 and is subject to change. Please consult individual program websites for the most up to date information._

## UCSB Programs

_**UCSB offers a variety of research-focused programs that provide mentorship, training, and professional development opportunities for students. This section highlights programs whose representatives would be joining [the SOAR² session, name TBD], pending availability. For a more comprehensive list of research opportunities at UCSB, please visit the UCSB Undergraduate Research website.**_

* [**The Center for Science and Engineering Partnerships (CSEP)**](https://csep.ucsb.edu/programs) offers several research programs for undergraduate students, including
    * [EUREKA! Scholars Program](https://eureka.csep.ucsb.edu/) for UCSB undergraduate STEM students
    * [MARC Scholars Program](https://marc.csep.ucsb.edu/student-scholars) for UCSB undergraduate students interested in pursuing biomedical research at the graduate level

* [**The McNair Scholars Program**](https://mcnair.ucsb.edu/) prepares first-generation and low-income UCSB undergraduate students interested in pursuing PhD studies in all fields of study.

* [**The Materials Research Laboratory**](https://www.mrl.ucsb.edu/education/undergraduate-opportunities/overview) hosts a number of research programs during the academic year and over the summer for UCSB (and external) students, including
    * [California Alliance for Maximizing Potential (CAMP)](https://www.mrl.ucsb.edu/education/undergraduate-opportunities/camp) for UCSB undergraduate students in STEM majors
    * [Future Leaders in Advanced Materials (FLAM)](https://www.mrl.ucsb.edu/education/undergraduate-opportunities/flam) for science and engineering students
    * [Research Internships in Science and Engineering (RISE)](https://www.mrl.ucsb.edu/education/undergraduate-opportunities/rise) for UCSB undergraduate students in STEM majors

* [**The Summer Undergraduate Research Fellowship (SURF)**](https://ccs.ucsb.edu/student-life/summer) offers students in the College of Creative Studies opportunities to engage in research, create original works or art, or develop entrepreneurial projects with mentorship from UCSB faculty. 

* [**The UC Leadership Excellence through Advanced Degrees (UC LEADS)**](https://www.graddiv.ucsb.edu/uc-leads) is a 24-month program of scientific research and graduate school preparation guided by individual faculty mentors.

## External

_**In addition to opportunities at UCSB, many government agencies, professional organizations, and universities offer research programs for undergraduate students. These experiences often take place during the summer, though some programs throughout the the academic year. Application deadlines for summer programs frequently occur between late fall and early winter. Thus, we encourage students to begin exploring opportunities well in advance. Many of these programs also provide housing, travel support, and stipends.**_

* The Department of Energy (DOE) hosts [**the Science Undergraduate Laboratory Internships (SULI)**](https://science.osti.gov/wdts/suli), which provides STEM students with research experience at a DOE laboratory under the guidance of laboratory staff scientists or engineers. Internships are typically 10 consecutive weeks during the summer or 16 consecutive weeks during the school year.

* [**Environmental Science Internships**](https://www.environmentalscience.org/internships) across government agencies, non-profit organizations, and private companies provide hands-on experience in research, field work, etc related to environmental issues.

* [**The NIH Summer Internship Program (SIP)**](https://www.training.nih.gov/research-training/pb/sip/) for students interested in exploring careers in research and healthcare. These are full-time paid summer research positions within one of the NIH Institutes and Centers in the NIH Intramural Research Program (IRP). NIH also has an [Academic Internship Program (AIP)](https://www.training.nih.gov/research-training/pb/aip_pb/) for conducting research in the NIH IRP during the school year. 

* [**The NSF Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)**](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/reu) supports research programs for undergraduate students. They have a [directory of all REU](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/initiatives/reu/search) sites that allows for search by research area, location, and keywords. 
* UCSB is home to two REU programs: the [Future Leaders in Advanced Materials (FLAM) Program](https://www.mrl.ucsb.edu/education/undergraduate-opportunities/flam) and the [Summer Research in Physics and Astronomy Program](https://reu.physics.ucsb.edu/).

* [**The Summer Research Early Identification Program (SR-EIP)**](https://theleadershipalliance.org/summer-research-early-identification-program) through the Leadership Alliance offers fully paid summer internships. These programs aim to provide undergraduate students with training and mentoring in conducting research and prepare them to pursue competitive applications to PhD or MD-PhD programs.

* Many academic/research institutions host summer research programs for undergraduate students in various disciplines. Below are some examples:
    * UCLA hosts [**Summer Programs for Undergraduate Research (SPUR)**](https://grad.ucla.edu/admissions/diversity/summer-programs-for-undergraduate-research-spur/), a number of which are open to students from outside UCLA
    * [**The HKUST Summer Undergraduate Research Program**](https://summercampus.hkust.edu.hk/hkust-summer-ug-research-program) (Hong Kong) offers students to take up research placement over the summer at HKUST, working on HKUST research projects and being immersed in local Hong Kong culture. 
    * [**The WAVE Fellows Program**](https://sfp.caltech.edu/undergraduate-research/programs/wavefellows) (Caltech, Pasadena, CA) is a 10-week summer research program for undergraduate students seriously considering attending graduate school

## Post-baccalaureate (post-bac) Programs

_**Post-baccalaureate, or post-bac, programs provides recent graduates with opportunities to gain additional research experience and professional development before pursuing graduate studies or other research careers. These programs typically last one to two years and are increasingly used as a bridge between undergraduate education and advanced training. Students may consider pursuing post-bac opportunities to strengthen research skills, explore career paths, build mentoring relationships, or become more competitive applicants for graduate and professional programs.**_

* [**American Chemical Society Bridge Programs**](https://www.broadinstitute.org/students/broad-biomedical-post-baccalaureate-scholars-program-bbps) include 1-2-year post-bac opportunities for students interested in pursuing careers in the chemical sciences.

* [**The NIH Post-bac Program**](https://www.training.nih.gov/research-training/pb/pb/) provides recent college graduates to perform full-time research for 1-2 years in the Intramural Research Program at NIH.

* A number of [**Leadership Alliance institutions** host post-bac programs](https://theleadershipalliance.org/resource/post-baccalaureate-programs). These programs aim to develop students’ research skills and academic competitiveness for PhD programs 

* Many academic/research institutions host post-bac programs in various disciplines. Below are some examples:
    * [**The Broad Biomedical Post-baccalaureate Scholars Program**](https://www.broadinstitute.org/students/broad-biomedical-post-baccalaureate-scholars-program-bbps) (Broad Institute, Cambridge, MA) is a two-year research program for students interested in pursuing a graduate degree and biomedical research
    * [**The Buck Postbaccalaureate Research Program**](https://www.buckinstitute.org/education-training/undergraduate-postbacc/) (Buck Institute, Novato, CA) provides two years of research experience in preparation for advanced degree programs or the biotech industry 
    * Yale University offers a couple of [**Post-Baccalaureate Research Education Programs (PREP)**](https://gsas.yale.edu/programs-of-study/post-baccalaureate-programs), including one with a focus on the history of art and a another on cultural heritage
