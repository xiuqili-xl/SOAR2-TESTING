title: Middle Eastern Studies Librarian
bio: Shima Moradi is the Middle Eastern Studies Librarian and Slavic, Area Studies Collection Strategist; she is also the Interim Open Access & STEM Strategist at the UCSB Library. Throughout her career as a Scholarly Communication Librarian, Researcher, and Assistant Professor of Library and Information Science, she has been a dedicated advocate for equitable knowledge ecosystems. She earned her BA, MA, and PhD in Library and Information Science, specializing in research evaluation, scientometrics, and open science. Shima has collaborated in open access initiatives internationally and overseen national projects focused on smart cities, open science policymaking, open data sharing, and the evaluation of higher education institutions. Her extensive publications and books cover open science frameworks, science policymaking, open access sustainability, and altmetrics. Her core research interests center on the transformation of scholarly communication through open science, open access repositories, research ethics, academic integrity, and leveraging technology to make science more transparent and accessible.
